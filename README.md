# Python to Java: A FinTech Reality Check

During my final years of Computer Engineering, I lived in the utopian, dynamically-typed paradise of Python. My thesis was in AI. Life was simple. Variables figured themselves out, lists held whatever you threw at them, and you could build a functioning application in about twelve lines of code.

Then, I graduated and joined a multinational FinTech vendor.

It turns out the global financial system doesn't run on `import money`. It runs on Java. It runs on millions of lines of strictly encapsulated, statically typed, heavily audited Java, maintained by thousands of developers across different time zones. I quickly realized that if I ever want to step up and become a systems architect, I have to learn to embrace the boilerplate.

This repository is my personal transition zone. It contains a series of AI-generated exercises designed to forcibly rewire my brain from Python's "do whatever you want" philosophy to Java's "please sign these three permission slips to instantiate an object" architecture. 

### What is this?
A collection of practice files where I am learning the hard way:
* Why everything suddenly needs a `public` getter and setter.
* Why I can no longer use a simple `==` to see if two words are the same.
* Why tracking money requires importing a massive `BigDecimal` library instead of just using a standard float.
* The crushing reality of fixed-size arrays.

The goal is to survive the compilation errors and eventually write robust enterprise code. Send help, or at least a caught `NullPointerException`.
